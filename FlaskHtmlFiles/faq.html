Frequently Asked Questions

What is E-waste?

E-waste is basically anything that contains electrical components. This includes items like remote control cars, mobile phones, laptops, batteries, and other electronic devices.

What Qualifies As E-Waste?

E-Waste includes a wide range of electronic items at the end of their life cycle:

  • IT Equipment: Servers, Desktop CPUs, Laptops, Monitors, Printers, Scanners, Keyboards, etc.
  • Domestic Appliances: Fridges, Washing machines, ACs, TVs, Audio equipment, Fans, etc.
  • Consumer Electronics: Cell-phones, Tablets, Cameras, etc.
  • Other Items: LED fixtures, Electrical wires, Cables, Circuit boards

What is IT hardware destruction?

IT hardware destruction is a secure process for destroying data on electronic devices like laptops and hard drives. Companies provide destruction certificates as proof that data has been completely and securely erased.

Why is E-waste management important?

E-waste management is crucial for:

  • Preventing toxic materials from reaching landfills
  • Reducing greenhouse gas emissions
  • Protecting environmental and human health
  • Recovering valuable materials for recycling
